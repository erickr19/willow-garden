Gardening Tips

Want to start digging but don't know where to begin?

Light:

Most veggies that bear fruit need at least 8 hours of sunlight. Leafy crops and herbs are grown in less light.

Size:

Beginners should use a small plot for the first year or two. A small plot still provides a suitable space to grow a handful of crops.

Soil:

Healthy rich soil is the key to a successful garden! A soil test can give you the information you need to provide the right nutrients to your soil.

Plants:

4-5 types of plants are suggested for your first garden. Cramming too many plants in one space will result in a smaller yield, not a bigger one. Succession planting, or planting right after a harvest, allows you to stretch your harvest season for the longest possible time.

Bugs:

Bugs such as bees, butterflies, tachinid flies, ladybugs, and more are actually beneficial to your garden. Including clumps of insect friendly plants such as sweet alyssum, zinnias, cosmos, and sunflowers will attract these friends.

Water, weed, and feed:

Newly seeded beds will need frequent watering while established crops can get by with one or two inches of water per week. Mulching the soil will help conserve water and reduce the need to irrigate. Mulching with several inches of straw or shredded leaves is a good start. Quick growing crops do not need fertilizer as much as long term crops.
